## How Insurance Pricing Works

### The Three Layers of Cost

Every insurance premium has three components:

1. **Claims** — What your employees actually use. Doctor visits, prescriptions, surgeries, hospitalizations. This is typically 75-85% of your total premium.

2. **Administration** — The carrier's cost to process claims, manage the network, handle customer service, and maintain compliance. Usually 10-15%.

3. **Profit and Risk Margin** — What the carrier charges for taking on the risk of your group. This is where the leverage exists.

When a carrier quotes you a renewal, they're pricing all three layers. Most brokers negotiate on the total number. We break it apart and address each layer independently.

### Why Renewals Go Up

Your renewal isn't random. It's driven by:
- **Your claims experience** — What your group actually cost the carrier last year
- **Medical trend** — Healthcare inflation (typically 6-8% annually)
- **Plan design** — Deductibles, copays, out-of-pocket maximums
- **Demographics** — Age and gender mix of your workforce
- **Network** — Which hospitals and doctors are in your plan

If your claims were high, your renewal goes up. If they were low, you might still see an increase because of medical trend. Understanding this gives you power in the negotiation.

## Funding Strategies

### Fully Insured
The traditional model. You pay a fixed premium per employee per month. The carrier takes all the risk. If claims are low, the carrier keeps the profit. If claims are high, the carrier absorbs the loss.

**Best for:** Small groups (under 50 employees), groups with unpredictable claims, risk-averse employers.

### Level Funded
A hybrid model. You pay a fixed monthly amount that includes expected claims, stop-loss insurance, and administration. If actual claims come in under the expected amount, you get a refund. If they come in over, the stop-loss insurance covers the excess.

**Best for:** Groups with 25-200 employees, healthy populations, employers who want potential savings without full risk exposure.

### Self-Insured (Self-Funded)
You pay claims directly as they occur, plus stop-loss insurance for catastrophic claims. No carrier profit margin on the base claims. You see every dollar — full transparency.

**Best for:** Groups with 100+ employees, stable claims history, employers who want maximum control and transparency.

## The Monte Carlo Simulation

This is where it gets interesting. Instead of looking at one renewal quote and hoping it's accurate, we run a Monte Carlo simulation on your benefits data.

### What Is Monte Carlo?

Monte Carlo simulation runs thousands of scenarios using your actual data — your claims history, your demographics, your plan design — and models what your costs could look like under different funding strategies and market conditions.

Instead of one number, you get a probability distribution. You can see:
- The **most likely** outcome (50th percentile)
- The **best case** (10th percentile)
- The **worst case** (90th percentile)
- How different funding strategies perform across all scenarios

### Why This Matters

Most brokers show you two or three quotes and say "pick one." We show you the range of possible outcomes across thousands of simulations. You're not guessing — you're making a decision based on probabilities.

This is the difference between "I think this will save you money" and "here's the probability distribution of your costs under each option."
