# Understanding Your Insurance Costs — The Cost Presentation

## What This Meeting Is About

You've done the fact finder. You've been through the education session. Now it's time for the numbers.

This is the cost presentation — where we put real dollars on the table and walk you through your options. No surprises, no hidden fees, no fine print. You'll see exactly what each option costs, what it covers, and how it compares to what you have today.

## How We Build Your Proposal

Your proposal isn't a generic quote pulled off a shelf. It's built from your data:

- **Your claims history** — What your group actually cost over the last 2-3 years
- **Your census** — The specific demographics of your workforce
- **Your plan design preferences** — What you told us matters to your employees
- **Market conditions** — What carriers are competitive for your group right now
- **Funding strategy** — Based on the Monte Carlo analysis, which approach fits your risk tolerance

We go to market with your data and negotiate with multiple carriers. We don't represent one carrier — we represent you.

### Cost Breakdown

For each option, we break the cost into its components:
- **Claims cost** — Expected claims based on your history and demographics
- **Fixed costs** — Administration, stop-loss (if applicable), network access
- **Employer contribution** — What you pay
- **Employee contribution** — What your employees pay
- **Total program cost** — The full picture

### Savings Analysis

If there are savings to be had, we'll show you exactly where they come from:
- Funding strategy change (e.g., moving from fully insured to level funded)
- Plan design adjustments (higher deductible with HSA funding)
- Carrier change (better rates for your demographics)
- Contribution strategy (restructuring how costs are shared)

We'll also show you what you're giving up with each option, if anything. Cheaper isn't always better — we want you to make an informed decision, not just a cheap one.

## Employee Impact

Changing benefits affects your employees. We model the employee impact for each option:

- **Paycheck impact** — How employee premiums change per pay period
- **Out-of-pocket exposure** — How deductibles and copays compare
- **Network changes** — Will employees need to change doctors?
- **Prescription coverage** — Any formulary differences?

If an option saves the company money but hurts employees, we'll tell you. If an option costs a little more but dramatically improves coverage, we'll show you that too.

## Implementation Timeline

If you decide to make a change, here's what the transition looks like:

1. **Decision** — You select the option that fits
2. **Enrollment** — We help you communicate changes to employees and manage open enrollment
3. **Effective date** — Aligned with your plan renewal date
4. **Ongoing management** — We handle the carrier relationship, compliance, and day-to-day questions

We don't disappear after the sale. The service relationship is where the real value is — and that's what our next conversation is about.
